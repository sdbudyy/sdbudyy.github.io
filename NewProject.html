How I Solve My Projects

Brainstorm

Whenever I task myself with a project in mind, my first step is to brainstorm all the factors I would have to think about: what my machine should be capable of achieving, cost, and contingency planning. I carefully consider the requirements and constraints, envisioning the end goal and the challenges that might arise. This thorough planning ensures that each project is both innovative and feasible, allowing me to create effective solutions that meet real-world needs.

Design

After getting a better understanding of the problem, I move on to design. In design, I specialize in creating on-paper drawings with a strong relation to the outside world, keeping in mind material strength, functionality, and practicality. I ensure that each component is meticulously planned to withstand real-world conditions and perform optimally. This step is crucial for visualizing the final product and identifying any potential issues before moving on to the development phase.

Calculate

Finally comes the actual calculation of the project: the dimensions, the wiring, the voltage required, the coding, and the price. This phase involves precise measurements and detailed planning to ensure every aspect of the project is accounted for and optimized. I meticulously calculate each element to ensure the final product is efficient, cost-effective, and meets all specified requirements. This comprehensive approach allows me to bring innovative ideas to life.
